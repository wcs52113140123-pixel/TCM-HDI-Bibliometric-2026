## Figure 5a. Herb family × Target family co-occurrence heatmap

Herb family × target family co-occurrence heatmap derived from the T2
herb–target layer (n = 1,725 mechanism-annotated records collapsed into
164 family–family pairs; data: `herb_family_x_target_family.parquet`).
Cell values are record counts; the YlOrRd color scale reflects record
density. The dominant lane is Hypericaceae → `cytochrome_P450` (69
records, the *Hypericum perforatum* CYP3A4 canon), followed by Fabaceae
→ `cytochrome_P450` (61, multi-source: *Glycyrrhiza*, *Astragalus*,
*Sophora*, *Pueraria*). Lamiaceae shows a secondary `ABC_transporter`
preference (25) reflecting *Salvia* / *Scutellaria* P-gp literature.

**Schema note on rows.** `flavonoid_compound` groups records whose herb
entity is a pure phytochemical that could not be unambiguously mapped
to a single botanical family (e.g., quercetin, baicalein, resveratrol).
`TCM_formula` groups records whose herb entity is a multi-herb
prescription (e.g., Xiao Chai Hu Tang, Liu Wei Di Huang Wan, Shenmai
injection). These are fallback canonical buckets under Schema v3
(see Methods §2.5) and should be interpreted at a different ontological
level than Linnaean botanical families.

**Schema note on columns.** The empty `oncology_target` column reflects
schema design rather than literature absence — antineoplastic
interactions are captured along the orthogonal `drug_class` axis
(Figure 6) as `antineoplastic_kinase_inhibitor`,
`antineoplastic_taxane`, `antineoplastic_anthracycline`, etc., while
their molecular-level effects on CYP and transporter targets appear
in the `cytochrome_P450` and `ABC_transporter` columns above.

## Figure 6. Mechanism Sankey: herb family → mechanism → drug class

Three-layer Sankey diagram of herb family → mechanism → drug class
flows, derived from the 900 gold-standard 3-axis mechanism triples
(T3 knowledge-graph layer; data: `kg_chain_summary.parquet`,
344 herb-family-to-drug-class chains). Flow width is proportional to
the number of supporting records; flows with fewer than 2 records are
suppressed for clarity.

The middle layer enumerates the eight Schema v3 mechanism categories
(`CYP_inhibition`, `CYP_induction`, `P-gp_inhibition`, `UGT_inhibition`,
`transporter_modulation`, `receptor_synergism`,
`organ_toxicity_modulation`, `signaling_pathway_modulation`), with
`other` reserved for confidence-validated but unclassified mechanisms.
Drug-class labels follow an ATC-aligned nomenclature with
mechanism-of-action subcategories for antineoplastics; this axis is
orthogonal to the molecular `target_family` axis used in Figure 5a.

Dominant flows: `Hypericaceae → CYP_induction → multi-drug-class`
captures the *Hypericum perforatum* (SJW) canonical literature
(19 flows to `other`, 8 to benzodiazepine, 4 to immunosuppressant).
`Schisandraceae → CYP_inhibition → immunosuppressant` (11 records)
captures the Wuzhi capsule – tacrolimus clinical niche.
`Lamiaceae → transporter_modulation → lipid_lowering_statin` (5)
captures the *Salvia*–statin interaction sub-literature.

## Figure 8. Temporal evolution of gold-standard mechanism triples (2005–2025)

Stacked area chart of the 900 gold-standard mechanism triples by
extraction year (mechanism category color-coded per Schema v3;
data: `kg_triples.parquet`). The 2014 peak (n = 98 triples) reflects
the early-to-mid 2010s surge in regulatory and clinical reports on
*Hypericum perforatum* (CYP3A4 induction) and *Schisandra*-derived
herbs (CYP3A4 inhibition; Wuzhi capsule clinical literature).
Secondary peaks at 2016 (72) and 2020 (62) align with subsequent
network-pharmacology and post-marketing safety waves.

Schema v3 mechanism categories `organ_toxicity_modulation` and
`signaling_pathway_modulation` appear predominantly from 2015 onward,
mirroring the paradigm shift toward network-pharmacology and
systems-toxicology frameworks identified in the keyword analysis
(Day 4 results; ref. paper §3.4).

Counts for 2024–2025 are systematically underestimated due to PubMed
indexing lag (corpus collected May 2026; see Methods §2.1) and are
excluded from temporal slope estimation in §3.6.
